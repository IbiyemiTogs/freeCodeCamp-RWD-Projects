Tribute to Dr. Stella Adadevoh

Dr. Ameyo Stella Shade Adadevoh

A Female Physician who curbed the wider spread of deadly Ebola Virus in her Country - Nigeria.

Image of Stella Adadevoh


Here's a timeline of Dr Adadevoh's life:

  • 1956 - Born in Lagos,Nigeria.

  • 1961 to 1962 - Attended Mainland Preparatory Primary School in Yaba, Lagos for Preschool.

  • 1964 to 1968 - Attended Primary school at Mainland Preparatory Primary School in Yaba, Lagos.

  • 1969 to 1974 - Attended Queens School Ibadan for her Secondary School Education.

  • 1975 to 1980 - Attended University of Lagos College of Medicine and finished with a Bachelor of Medicine/Bachelor of Surgery Degree.

  • 1983 - She She spent her residency at Lagos University Teaching Hospital West African College of Physicians and Surgeons and then went to London to complete her fellowship in endocrinology at Hammersmith Hospital.

  • 1986 - Ameyo Adadevoh married Afolabi Emmanuel Cardoso.

  • 1993 to 2014 - She spent 21 years at the First Consultants Medical Center in Lagos, Nigeria. There, she served as the Lead Consultant Physician and Endocrinologist.

  • July 2014 - Dr. Adadevoh correctly diagnosed Liberian Patrick Sawyer as Nigeriaâ€™s first case of Ebola at First Consultant Hospital in Lagos, Nigeria. Also, she kept Patrick Sawyer at the hospital despite receiving a request from a Liberian ambassador to release him from the hospital. Dr. Adadevoh tried to create an isolation area, despite the lack of protective equipment, by creating a wooden barricade outside Patrick Sawyer's door. Her heroic effort saved the nation from widespread infection.

  • August 2014 - Dr Adadevoh succumbed to the Ebola Virus Disease whilst in quarantine and passed away on the 19 August 2014 in Lagos, Nigeria.

Dr Adadevoh's selflessness and passsion for humanity ensured that the Ebola virus didnt become an epidemic. She has received several posthumous awards for this heroic act.
